## Findings — H1 confirmed, cleanly

| Operating point | v2 (orig.) | **v3 re-vote** | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Restrictive-gated** (restrict × veto ≡ unanimous = balanced × veto) | 82% catch / 26% FR | **81% catch / 18% FR** | catch −1pt (noise), **FR −8pt** |
| **Everyone-can-block** (balanced × unanimous) | 85% catch / 34% FR | **89% catch / 26% FR** | **catch +4pt, FR −8pt** |

- **Both v3 operating points Pareto-dominate their v2 counterparts.** Restrictive-gated
  holds catch (81 vs 82, inside the CI [71–88]) and cuts FR by 8pt; everyone-can-block
  improves on *both* axes. Net discrimination margin **+56% → +64%**.
- The −8pt FR drop matches the analytic prediction (content_moderator alone fired 8 of
  the not-owned FR firings; removing it + tightening counter_extremism/CONSUM).
- **H4 holds** — fully-owned catch flat across subtlety (75/75/88/75/94, no decay
  toward the veiled end); not-owned FR does not rise toward the blunt L5 end
  (25/25/12/12/12). No surface-signal slope introduced. partial-owned rises with
  subtlety as before (sensible).
- Catch is still carried by `consumer_protection` (unchanged) — the persona swap
  changed precision, not the catch engine, exactly as designed.

## Caveats

- Compared against v2's **original** numbers, not a matched re-vote control, so a
  sliver of the FR drop could be vote-resampling noise — but the −8pt matches the
  prediction and catch holding at 81% argues sampling is stable. The matched control
  (re-vote the v2 committee on the same candidates) is the airtight version, deferred.
- **H2 (harassment *recovery*) is NOT tested here** — it needs the new fully-owned
  doxxing/threat scenarios from the v3 corpus. partial-owned block-rate fell 61%→51%
  (descriptive; content_moderator's indiscriminate firing gone), as expected.
