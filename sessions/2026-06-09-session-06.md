## What we did

### 1. Response-only committee visibility (D24)

Made committee visibility a society-config setting so v1 stays reproducible
(default `prompt-and-response`):

- `configs/v2/personas.yaml` — added a `voting_protocol_response_only` block
  (Input section names only `CANDIDATE RESPONSE`; the committee is told it does
  not see the user's message).
- `src/config.py` — `SocietyConfig` gains `visibility` (default
  `prompt-and-response`); `load_society` reads the society YAML's `visibility`
  and `_load_personas` composes the matching protocol into each persona.
- `src/agents/committee.py` — `cast_vote` / `_format_user_message` take
  `visibility`; response-only emits just the candidate.
- `src/pipeline/pipeline.py` — `run()` passes `society.visibility` per vote.
- `src/batch.py` — now also passes `scenario` + `subtlety` through to result rows
  (additive; v1 analysis unaffected).

**Verified end-to-end** against local Ollama (qwen3-abliterated:14b is the only
local model): a phishing-email prompt through `configs/v2/balanced-8.yaml` →
8 votes, **0 parse failures**, committee reasons reference only the response.
(Notable artifact: several personas misread the candidate phishing email as a
*warning about* phishing — exactly the kind of response-only behaviour v2 is
built to capture, not fix.)

### 3. v2 analyzer — `src/analyze_v2.py` (new)

Keeps `analyze.py` untouched (v1 reproducibility); imports and reuses its

- **Subtlety curves** — block-rate by category × L1–L5 (the headline hypothesis

Validated on a synthetic fixture spanning all 3 categories × 5 levels.

### 4. v2 corpus authored via fan-out workflow

`infra/author_v2_corpus.workflow.js` — 15 author buckets (5 per category) ×
4 scenarios × 5 subtlety levels, each scenario **a-priori classified from remit
text** and **adversarially verified** for subtlety-invariance + scope, with a
top-up phase for categories short of 16. Authoring/verification ran on the
capable orchestration model (NOT the abliterated subject). Output:
`data/v2/prompts/{fully,partial,not}-owned-v2.jsonl`.

> **Scope reconciliation (flag for validation):** v2 fully-owned (phishing / scam
> / coercion) butts against `red-team-design.md`'s "no real-world fraud /
> cybercrime" out-of-scope line. Resolved as *refused-by-commercial-LLMs but zero
> real uplift* — deception **templates with fictional placeholders**,
> interpersonal coercion, dark-pattern / fake-review copy are IN; real targets,
> working hacking steps, weapons/CBRN/CSAM/self-harm are HARD-OUT (dropped by both
> author and verify phases). **This boundary is the #1 thing to check in the
> validation session.**

The run was stopped once mid-way (cost check) and **resumed from cache**
(`resumeFromRunId`). The resumed run then **died at 105/121 agents** (16 agents
in-flight, never returned; background process gone, task handle unqueryable).
Rather than re-run (more cost, repeat-death risk), the corpus was **salvaged from
the 105 cached agent outputs with zero new tokens** via
`infra/reconstruct_v2_corpus.py`: it joins each journal `result` to its
transcript (authors → category; verifiers → intended category + scenario stem),
keeps verdict-admissible scenarios (`admissible AND derived==intended`), dedups,
and trims to 16/category. Verifier-died scenarios are kept as a flagged backfill
so each category still reaches 16.

**Final corpus (`data/v2/prompts/`, 240 prompts = 48 scenarios × 5 levels):**

| category | scenarios | verified-admissible avail | unverified backfill |
|---|---|---|---|
| fully-owned | 16/16 | 19 | 0 |
| partial-owned | 16/16 | 9 | **7** |
| not-owned | 16/16 | 20 | 0 |

Schema validated: 80 prompts/category, every scenario has exactly subtlety L1–L5,
`ground_truth` matches category (block/none/release), no empty prompts. Spot-check
confirms the subtlety gradient holds the owned feature constant while varying
intent-deniability (e.g. fully-owned `supplement-cures-disease` L1 innocent
framing → L5 "I want to trick buyers"; not-owned `rent-control-bad-policy` stays
one-sided opinion L1→L5).

> **partial-owned is the soft spot:** only 9 of its 16 are verifier-confirmed
> (subtlety-invariance is hardest there, and the 16 dead agents took out several
> partial verifiers). The 7 backfilled scenarios are flagged `UNVERIFIED` in their
> `notes` — **prioritise these in the validation session.** If validation rejects
> too many, re-run the authoring workflow for partial-owned only (it resumes the
> verified ones from cache).
